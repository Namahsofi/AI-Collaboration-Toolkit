# Product Learnings

## Overview

ACT is designed to help mission-driven organizations collaborate with AI responsibly, not simply generate content faster.

Through testing and interview preparation, several important product learnings emerged.

---

## 1. AI Output Needs Verification

A key risk in AI-assisted grant writing is that AI may generate confident-sounding facts, statistics, or impact claims that the organization cannot verify.

ACT should treat unverified claims as not ready for submission.

Future versions should clearly flag:

- Unverified statistics
- Unsupported impact claims
- Missing budget details
- Missing outcome measurement methods
- Unconfirmed funder requirements

---

## 2. Review Should Create Helpful Friction

A simple checklist may not be enough when users are under deadline pressure.

ACT should make risky content harder to submit by adding helpful friction before export or final use.

Examples:

- Highlight unverified claims
- Require final human approval before export
- Warn users when budget or outcome data is missing
- Separate AI-generated language from verified organizational facts

---

## 3. Minimum Safe Review Mode

Users may not always have time for a full review process.

Instead of allowing users to turn review off, ACT could offer a “Minimum Safe Review” mode.

Minimum Safe Review would quickly check:

- Budget numbers
- Timeline
- Unverified claims
- Funder requirements
- Final human approval

This helps users move faster without removing responsible safeguards.

---

## 4. PDF Export Matters

Testing showed that the final PDF export is an important part of the user experience.

A useful AI workflow should not only generate text. It should help users produce a clean, readable document they can review, share, and improve.

Future improvements may include:

- Cleaner formatting
- Better tables
- Export warnings for unverified content
- Review summary included at the end of the PDF

---

## 5. Saved Work and Project History

The current MVP saves progress locally in the browser.

Future versions could add local saved drafts so users can return to previous projects.

Potential features:

- Draft name
- Saved drafts list
- Load previous draft
- Delete draft
- Duplicate draft for a new proposal

This should begin as a local-only feature before adding accounts or databases.

---

## 6. Accountability Model

ACT follows a simple accountability model:

**Claude drafts. ACT guides. Humans verify.**

Claude can help organize and draft content, but the organization remains responsible for facts, numbers, claims, and final submission decisions.

---

## Product Direction

ACT should continue evolving from a grant proposal workflow into a broader library of AI Collaboration Workflows for mission-driven organizations.

Potential future workflows:

- Board meeting preparation
- Impact reporting
- Donor communication
- Education support
- Small business planning
- Workforce development workflows
