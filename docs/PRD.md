# Executive Summary

AI Collaboration Toolkit (ACT) introduces a structured methodology for human-AI collaboration, expressed through software as reusable AI Collaboration Workflows (ACWs).

Every ACW follows the ACT Framework:

**Discover → Prepare → Collaborate → Review → Refine → Reuse**

The framework—not any particular application—is the core intellectual property of ACT.

Instead of giving users an empty AI chat box, ACT guides them through structured collaboration processes that improve both the quality of the work produced and the user's ability to collaborate with AI over time.

Human judgment remains central throughout the process.

---

# Vision

Create a future where effective collaboration with AI is a learnable, transferable methodology available to every mission-driven organization.

ACT aims to reduce AI Capability Debt by replacing ad hoc AI use with repeatable, organization-owned collaboration practices.

Success means organizations become better AI collaborators—not simply better AI users.

---

# Mission

ACT exists because many organizations doing the world's most important work have the fewest resources to effectively use AI.

Our mission is to provide an open, repeatable methodology that helps organizations collaborate responsibly and effectively with AI while continuously improving their own capabilities.

Success is measured by increased human capability—not merely AI-generated output.

---

# Problem Statement

Most AI failures are collaboration failures rather than technology failures.

Current AI tools provide powerful models but little guidance on how people should work with them.

Organizations experience:

- Blank-chatbox syndrome
- Inconsistent prompting
- No repeatable methodology
- Heavy dependence on individual AI experts
- Limited institutional learning
- Weak review processes
- Growing AI Capability Debt

ACT addresses these challenges through structured collaboration workflows.

# ACT Framework

Every AI Collaboration Workflow (ACW) follows six stages:

1. Discover
2. Prepare
3. Collaborate
4. Review
5. Refine
6. Reuse

This methodology is platform-independent and represents the core intellectual property of ACT.

---

# MVP Workflow Collection

Initial release includes:

- Grant Proposal Development
- Board Meeting Preparation
- Impact Reporting
- Donor Communication

Each workflow teaches users the ACT Framework while completing meaningful work.
