# ACT Framework

## Overview

The ACT Framework is the core methodology behind the AI Collaboration Toolkit (ACT).

Unlike traditional AI tools that begin with an empty chat box, the ACT Framework guides users through a structured collaboration process that improves both the quality of AI-assisted work and the user's long-term AI collaboration skills.

The methodology is platform-independent and can be implemented in web applications, no-code platforms, desktop software, or future AI systems.

---

# The Six Stages

## 1. Discover

Understand the problem before asking AI.

Goals:

- Define the objective.
- Identify stakeholders.
- Gather background information.
- Clarify constraints.
- Understand success criteria.

Deliverable:

A clear understanding of the task.

---

## 2. Prepare

Collect everything needed for effective collaboration.

Goals:

- Gather documents.
- Organize context.
- Identify missing information.
- Complete a readiness assessment.

Deliverable:

A complete collaboration brief.

---

## 3. Collaborate

Work with AI using structured prompts and iterative refinement.

Goals:

- Generate ideas.
- Explore alternatives.
- Produce first drafts.
- Encourage reasoning.
- Improve thinking.

Deliverable:

Working AI-generated outputs.

---

## 4. Review

Human review is mandatory.

Goals:

- Verify facts.
- Check accuracy.
- Improve clarity.
- Detect hallucinations.
- Ensure alignment with objectives.

Deliverable:

Verified content.

---

## 5. Refine

Improve the reviewed work.

Goals:

- Strengthen writing.
- Improve structure.
- Remove unnecessary complexity.
- Increase quality.
- Address reviewer feedback.

Deliverable:

High-quality final work.

---

## 6. Reuse

Capture organizational knowledge.

Goals:

- Save reusable templates.
- Document lessons learned.
- Improve future workflows.
- Build institutional knowledge.

Deliverable:

Reusable AI Collaboration Workflow assets.

---

# Core Principles

- Human judgment always comes first.
- AI supports thinking rather than replacing it.
- Good prompts emerge from good processes.
- Every workflow teaches transferable skills.
- Learning compounds over time.
- Organizations should own their AI capability.

---

# Why the Framework Matters

Most AI failures are collaboration failures rather than technology failures.

The ACT Framework reduces AI Capability Debt by replacing ad hoc AI interactions with structured, repeatable collaboration practices.

Instead of teaching people how to write better prompts, ACT teaches them how to become better AI collaborators.

---

# Success

A successful ACT Framework implementation helps users:

- Produce higher-quality work.
- Make better decisions.
- Learn transferable AI collaboration skills.
- Create reusable organizational knowledge.
- Build long-term AI capability.
